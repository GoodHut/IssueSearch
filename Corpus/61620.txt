 URL : "https://github.com/softwaremill/bootzooka/issues/114" TITLE : better password hashing BODY : looking in user.scala ln 31-37 https://github.com/softwaremill/bootzooka/blob/master/backend/src/main/scala/com/softwaremill/bootzooka/user/domain/user.scala l31-l37 i can see you're using pbkdf2withhmacsha1 with 10,000 rotations. by my understanding, in 2016, sha1 is a pretty dated choice for this. at the very least, it should probably be sha256 .. though both sha256 and sha512 are vulnerable to length extension attacks. so sha384 would be a better choice, or even a sha-3 hash; if sticking to sha. other good alternatives to look into would be argon2 https://password-hashing.net/ argon2 winner of the password hashing competition or blake2 https://blake2.net/ combined in a proper way to be useful for password hashing. i believe argon uses a version of this anyway . this is a pretty good read on the subject: https://paragonie.com/blog/2016/02/how-safely-store-password-in-2016 other refs: http://stackoverflow.com/questions/19348501/pbkdf2withhmacsha512-vs-pbkdf2withhmacsha1
