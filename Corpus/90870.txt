 URL : "https://github.com/rkallis/project-review/issues/6" TITLE : subtle: function ordering BODY : https://github.com/rkallis/project-review/blob/master/server.js l53-l186 this is a very subtle point, but think about how you order your functions. to someone reading your code, does it make sense to read the comment-related functions first, before even reading how to get the user or create an article? the order of your functions affects the readability of the project, so it's worth putting thought into. i would put the functions you've written after the ones that were provided to you, because comments don't exist without both user and articles, so it makes sense that they'd come later in the order. by doing this you might also have noticed the downvote and upvote functions written for you and been able to use those to your advantage.
