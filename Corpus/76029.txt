 URL : "https://github.com/librosa/librosa/issues/612" TITLE : improve util.valid_audio BODY : description in response to 611, we could be more careful about checking for well-formed audio input, in two ways: 1. verify the dtype of the input buffer. if you pass in int -typed data, you may have a bad time. 2. for multi-channel audio, verify that the channels dim and time dim are in the right place. this is tricky in general, but a good heuristic would be y.shape 1 >= y.shape 0 .
