 URL : "https://github.com/vaultedpages/vaultedpages/issues/9" TITLE : encrypt files and metadata BODY : gophercon 2016 video by george tankersley https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=2r_kmzxb74w explaining best practices for encryption in golang. example and recent notes https://github.com/gtank/cryptopasta are referenced here. basic summary - aes: one of the few options for good, modern encryption, but this option is often hardware accelerated, making it generally the best option. - gcm: only block cipher mode that provides authenticated encryption - hash: sha2 good or 3 which is mostly just different - hmac: use this instead of a hash directly - signatures: ecdsa p256 implementation of which is well designed in go
