 URL : "https://github.com/dhuppenkothen/ShiftyLines/issues/18" TITLE : literature on line finding BODY : starting this issue to collect literature on line identification and modelling. good news: so far, i haven't found anyone who solves the problem we're trying to solve. i'll keep looking a bit more for relevant references, though. here are a few references i found: https://arxiv.org/abs/1109.3539 radio searches of hi absorption lines at different redshifts; not quite convinced by this paper; they mess up their inference in a couple of places, i think http://dl.acm.org/citation.cfm?id=1600989 much more complicated problem: decomposition of 3d astronomical data cubes into spectral line maps; uses an approach not too dissimilar to ours for the first line-finding part http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2000astro.ph..8170v old-school bayesian stats paper from 2001; has a lot of the basics of what we want, but fixes the number of lines and their positions http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/1999astro.ph..6394f line search strategies in grbs; lots of discussion about both frequentist + bayesian methods, but limited by the computational requirements, since the paper is from 1999 http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2014gid.....4..563l rosetta spectra!!! they're not quite the same as what we're looking at, but have similar issues. paper starts out well, but then never talks about how to estimate the number of lines n in their spectra, even though they mention it early on http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2016arxiv161110189t atomic physics! uses nested sampling, but uses bayes factors to compare models with different numbers of lines
